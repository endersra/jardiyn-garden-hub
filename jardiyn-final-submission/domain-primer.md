## Soil Types & Characteristics

| Type | Drainage | Nutrients | Best For | Amendment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Clay** | Poor | Rich | Heavy feeders | Add grit, compost |
| **Sandy** | Excellent | Low | Drought plants | Add organic matter |
| **Loam** | Ideal | Balanced | Most plants | Minimal amendment |
| **Peat** | Poor | Low/acidic | Blueberries, azaleas | Drain, raise pH |
| **Chalky** | High | Alkaline | Lavender, clematis | Add sulfur |

---

## Soil pH Guide

| pH | Type | Best Plants |
|---|---|---|
| <6.5 | Acidic | Blueberries, rhododendrons, azaleas |
| 6.5-7.0 | Neutral | Most vegetables, roses, lawn grass |
| >7.5 | Alkaline | Lavender, clematis, lilac, magnolia |

## Common Garden Pests & Organic Remedies

| Pest | Signs | Organic Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Aphids** | Sticky residue, curled leaves | Neem oil, ladybugs, strong water spray |
| **Japanese Beetles** | Skeletonized leaves | Milky spore, hand-pick, neem |
| **Slugs** | Irregular holes, slime trails | Beer traps, diatomaceous earth |
| **Late Blight** | Dark spots on tomato leaves | Remove affected tissue, copper fungicide |
| **Powdery Mildew** | White coating on leaves | Baking soda spray (1 tsp/gal water), improve airflow |
| **Fungus Gnats** | Wilting seedlings | Let soil dry out, yellow sticky traps |

**Principle:** Always prefer organic solutions first (sustainability mission).

## Invasive Species (Flag Immediately)

- **Kudzu** — Covers everything, southeastern US
- **Japanese Knotweed** — Spreads via rhizomes, very hard to eradicate
- **English Ivy** — In non-native regions, kills trees
- **Purple Loosestrife** — Replaces native wetland plants
- **Garlic Mustard** — Early spring invader

**Action:** Always recommend removal + native alternatives.

## Watering Rules

- **Most vegetables:** 1–1.5 inches/week
- **Overwatering is the #1 cause of plant death** (root rot)
- **Clay soil:** Water less frequently (retains moisture)
- **Sandy soil:** Water more frequently (drains fast)
- **Morning watering preferred** (reduces fungal disease)
- **2-inch soil test:** If dry 2 inches down, it's time to water

## Native Plants Principles

- Attract local pollinators (bees, butterflies)
- Require less water (adapted to climate)
- Support local food chains
- Resist local pests better
- Improve soil health

**Examples by Region:**
- **Northeast:** Black-eyed Susans, coneflowers, Joe-Pye weed
- **Midwest:** Purple coneflower, blazing star, bee balm
- **Southwest:** Desert rose, brittlebush, penstemon
- **Southeast:** Coral honeysuckle, native azaleas, ironweed
- **Pacific NW:** Oregon grape, sword fern, salal

---

## Sustainability Criteria

Every recommendation should consider:
1. **Native preference** (local ecosystem support)
2. **Water efficiency** (drought tolerance for zone)
3. **Organic pest management** (no synthetic chemicals)
4. **Soil health** (amendment, no tilling)
5. **Pollinator support** (nectar/pollen sources)
